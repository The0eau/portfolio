Airbus — GraphRAG for Technical Q&A

I worked as a Data Science / Product Management intern to design and evaluate a GraphRAG pipeline that answers questions on complex maintenance and technical documents. The system augments LLM retrieval with knowledge graphs to ground responses, reduce hallucinations, and make institutional knowledge easier to discover.

I led discovery → PRD → evaluation loops with engineers and stakeholders, and integrated LangChain, MLflow, and GCP Vertex AI to operationalize experiments and track model performance. I benchmarked internal approaches against external references (e.g., IBM and HKU projects) to validate design choices and guide roadmap trade-offs.

  • Outcomes: +50% improvement in QA accuracy and +70% increase in data accessibility measured in internal evaluations and stakeholder demos.
  • RAG design: entity/relation extraction → graph construction → retrieval over subgraphs → LLM generation with grounded citations.
  • Evaluation: curated gold sets, exact-match/semantic metrics, error taxonomy, and failure-mode analysis to prioritize fixes.
  • Scalability: designed for incremental graph updates and cost-aware inference on Vertex AI; tracked experiments with MLflow.
  • Stakeholders: authored PRDs, ran live demos, and delivered English presentations to senior engineers and cross-functional teams.

This project bridged user needs and engineering constraints: we translated ambiguous questions into reproducible retrieval tasks, instrumented quality and cost, and shipped a path to scale GraphRAG across additional document families and use cases.
