Sociological Study of the Bartender Profession

September 2023 - November 2023

As part of this sociological project, I conducted an in-depth study of the bartender profession, exploring its social, economic, and cultural dynamics. The main objective was to understand the various facets of this profession in terms of professional motivations, career trajectories, and social interactions within the restaurant industry.

Objectives

  • Analyze social and economic dynamics: Explore aspects such as management, customer interaction, and teamwork within the bartender profession.
  • Study professional trajectories: Examine the career paths, evolutions, and relationship of bartenders to their profession.
  • Explore the impact on social identity: Investigate the social roles and perception of the profession in society, focusing on interpersonal relationships and social roles.

Skills Developed

  • Conducting semi-structured interviews: Ability to conduct qualitative interviews with active listening and analyzing responses for meaningful social insights.
  • Qualitative and thematic data analysis: Processing interview responses to identify trends, themes, and insights into the bartender profession.
  • Sociological report writing: Writing a detailed report of the results, presenting conclusions on the impact of the profession within a social and cultural context.
  • Social and cultural sensitivity: Gaining an in-depth understanding of human interactions within the restaurant sector, particularly in social and cultural contexts.

Results Achieved

  • Identified the motivations and professional trajectories of bartenders, highlighting the challenges and opportunities within the profession.
  • Conducted a thorough analysis of social relationships and interpersonal dynamics within the bartender profession, including their impact on social identity.
  • Presented results in a clear, structured sociological report, offering a deeper understanding of the profession from a social perspective.

Key Skills

  • Workplace sociology
  • Qualitative interviews and thematic analysis
  • Report writing and presentation of results
  • Social and economic analysis
  • Field studies and participatory observation
